About Us

The Madrasa system in Kerala is a well-established and effective edu cational institution that plays a cru cial role in imparting basic religious education to the younger generation. With its clearly defined aims and ob jectives, structured curriculum, sylla bus, and necessary tools, it ensures a smooth and comprehensive ed ucational experience. Notably, this system is designed to allow students to pursue religious studies alongside their modern education in main stream schools. As a result, nearly every Muslim child in Kerala under goes madrasa education by the time they reach adolescence, highlighting its significance in the community. One of the fundamental princi ples of Islam is acquiring and sharing knowledge, which forms the core of Islamic civilization. Muslims across the globe have established various educational institutions at prima ry and higher levels to educate their communities on the essential prin ciples of the religion and produce knowledgeable scholars. Education, according to Islam, is a powerful tool for resulting positive social change and dispelling ignorance and su perstitions. The advent of Islam rev olutionized the world and laid the foundation for a glorious civilization and culture. In Kerala, the Madras sa education system brought about significant social change. While the structural form of Madrasas we see today did not exist during the time of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), a strong tradition of learning and teaching was already present. Kerala holds historical signifi cance as the land where the initial spread of Islam in India took place. The system of Islamic education likely began alongside the estab lishment of the first few mosques in Kerala. Over a period of a thousand years, this system evolved, surviving despite a century marked by a great er emphasis on modern education. Today, the Madrasa system in Ker ala thrives, incorporates new trends, and also enables Muslim children to attend both religious and regular schools simultaneously. This unique feature sets the Muslims of Kerala apart from their counterparts in In dia, as they have successfully fash ioned an education system that em braces both religious and secular knowledge. Kerala proudly boasts the highest literacy rate in India, and the Mus lim community in Kerala stands out as the most well-educated Mus lim population in the country. This achievement is attributed, in part, to the extensive network of educational institutions established by commu nity leaders across the state. These institutions, including Madrassas and other schools, have played a vital role in advancing education among Muslims in Kerala. Within the Malabar region of Ker ala, there are five distinct patterns of religious educational provisions for Muslims. First is the “Othupalli,” a single-teacher school system set up adjacent to mosques. Madrasas operate parallel to the general school system, providing education from primary to senior secondary levels. Boarding Madrassas offer residential facilities, combining Madrassa education with regular schooling for a fee covering education, boarding, and food. Last ly, Arabic colleges provide education in both Islamic and secular subjects at degree and postgraduate levels in residential campuses. The constitution of the Madrassa Board ensures the proper function ing and administration of Madrasas in Kerala. This governing body plays a critical role in formulating policies, monitoring the quality of education, and ensuring the overall develop ment of Madrassa students. In brief, the Madrassa system in Kerala serves as an efficient and inte gral educational platform for impart ing religious knowledge to the Muslim youth. Its harmonious integration with modern education allows stu dents to excel in both domains. With its rich history, impactful social transformation, and contribution to the high literacy rate among Muslims in Kerala, the Madrassa system stands as a testament to the power of education and the importance of fostering friendship and understanding among communities.
