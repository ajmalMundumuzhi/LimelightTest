Reflections of Life: The Power of Education, Hope and Personal Growth.

I still remember the profound words of a legendary figure in my dream. Wordsworth had long ago remarked that our lives are a reflection of the lives in heaven. While some might find that notion far-fetched, life re mains undeniably precious. If it weren’t, no one would make efforts to cling onto it as long as they can. The essence of life lies in its existence. Life includes existence, and without existence, life cannot occur. Howev er, existence can sometimes be challenging. There are many who do not have access to a good education, and there are those who lack the warmth of a mother’s love (apolo gies, Mom, for using your name to describe the most affectionate aspect, “college life”). Often, intellectuals can be harsh, but like existence, education is an essential feature. Another important aspect is hope. When darkness engulfs our lives, hope is what we hold onto. It is hope that paves the way for survival. Survival and hope are crucial for everyone when it comes to life and living. In a world driven by competition, survival becomes increasingly difficult, especially for AJMAL RAMUNATTUKARA those facing challenging circum stances. Hope becomes the only means of survival, and I believe this mindset has shaped my character positively. I began studying in my first year at Manhajurashad Islamic College Chelambra with the usual motiva tion of a ten-year-old: self-cen teredness. I was headstrong and embarked on a profound journey, driven by the question of what Arabic literature could do for me. It made me feel smart, interesting, and mysterious because very few had studied the language thoroughly. ...In my early days at MRIC in my hometown of Ramanattukara, the language seemed impossibly diffi cult, but it offered an escape from my mundane life in primary edu cation. Ironically, I ignored its prac ticality and focused on mastering Arabic, a beautiful language that opened up endless possibilities in foreign lands. However, what matters most now is the realization that Arabic can be used not only for my own benefit but also to serve others. ...In 2018, my knowledge of Arabic empowered me to assist my friends and family in their foreign employment journey. The process of learn ing, improving skills through litera ture, training, and other mediums, is akin to a mesmerizing display of fireworks in our intellectual growth. These endeavors help develop our talents and guide us towards pro fessions that align with our aspira tions and responsibilities. Education instills hope for a better life and enhances our foundational skills. It possesses a transformative power that can elevate us beyond our current circumstances. A productive mindset enables us to contribute positively to society. Education teaches us how to navi gate challenges and overcome them. A well-educated individual un derstands how to comport oneself with politeness and avoid causing offense. It teaches us to live a dis ciplined life while still maintaining respectability. Our future is built upon the foundation of education, a potent tool that can address numer ous issues. A well-educated person not only comprehends problems but also possesses the essential abilities to tackle them. These include dedi cation, attention, hard work, and the blessings of teachers. These ele ments form the amalgamation that will yield results in the days to come. Such thoughts encapsulate my jour ney as I dedicated seven years of my life to Manhaj. In the end, the realm of risk is the most innovative for me. It allows me to engage in meaningful activities and fosters groundbreaking ideas in the face of negativity. Each person I encounter is like a chapter and a distinct plot in my observations. It becomes a question of “to be or not to be,” a period in my life where it’s do or die. I have miles to go to fill the blank pages of my personality. To be honest... I believe I can do to portray another holistic and astonishing brief story of my hidden miracles.
